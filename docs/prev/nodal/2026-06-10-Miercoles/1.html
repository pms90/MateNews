El pasado 4 de junio los y las voluntarias lograron comunicarse con sus familias, luego de cuatro días de huelga de hambre. Durante ese contacto, la voluntaria argentina Paula Giménez describió la situación con una frase que resume la gravedad del caso: «Esto es un infierno».

Desde entonces, han transcurrido cinco días sin nuevas comunicaciones ni información oficial verificable sobre el estado de salud de las personas detenidas.

Según los testimonios transmitidos a sus familiares, los voluntarios permanecen aislados, sin contacto entre sí, y sostienen medidas de protesta frente a su situación de privación arbitraria de libertad. Las familias denunciaron episodios de deterioro físico, pérdida de peso, desmayos y la ausencia de acceso humanitario independiente.

Las personas detenidas son:

La presencia de ciudadanos de Argentina, Uruguay, España, Italia, Túnez, Portugal, Polonia y Estados Unidos convierte este caso en una preocupación humanitaria internacional que requiere la intervención urgente de los organismos competentes.

Durante la conferencia se brindará información actualizada sobre la situación humanitaria y los reclamos impulsados por las familias para obtener:

La ayuda humanitaria y la solidaridad no son delitos. Exigimos la intervención urgente de las cancillerías de los países involucrados, las Naciones Unidas, el Comité Internacional de la Cruz Roja y todos los organismos internacionales competentes.

INTERNATIONAL PRESS CONFERENCE | 16 Days Since the Abduction of Ten Humanitarian Volunteers in Libya

June 9, 2026

📅 Tuesday, June 9 – 5:00 PM (Argentina / UTC-3) 📍 Rodríguez Peña 80 – Buenos Aires City, Argentina 💻 In-person and online participation 📩 To request the connection link: +54 9 3512 09-2642 | +54 9 11 5873-4846

We invite media outlets, human rights organizations, international solidarity networks, journalists, and activists to participate in this international press conference to provide information on the situation of the ten humanitarian volunteers of the Global Sumud Maghreb Convoy, who were abducted in Libya while taking part in a civilian mission accompanying the delivery of humanitarian aid to Gaza.

This call takes place amid growing concern, as 16 days have passed since their detention and incommunicado confinement, without regular access to independent medical assistance or international humanitarian visits.

On June 4, the volunteers were able to communicate with their families after four days on hunger strike. During that contact, Argentine volunteer Paula Giménez described the situation with a phrase that reflects the gravity of the case:

«This is hell.»

Since then, five days have passed without any new communication or verifiable official information regarding the health condition of the detainees.

According to testimonies shared with their families, the volunteers remain isolated, unable to communicate with one another, and continue protest measures against their arbitrary deprivation of liberty. Families have reported physical deterioration, weight loss, fainting episodes, and the absence of independent humanitarian access.

The detained volunteers are:

🇦🇷 Lucas Aguilera (Argentina) 🇦🇷 Paula Giménez (Argentina) 🇺🇾 Matías Álvarez Rodríguez (Uruguay) 🇪🇸 Alicia Armesto Núñez (Spain) 🇮🇹 Domenico Centrone (Italy) 🇮🇹 Leonarda Alberizia (Italy) 🇵🇹 Ana Margarida França Santana Baptista (Portugal) 🇵🇱 Lauro Kwoczala (Poland) 🇹🇳 Ashraf Khoja (Tunisia) 🇺🇸 Jenelle Jones (United States)

The involvement of citizens from Argentina, Uruguay, Spain, Italy, Tunisia, Portugal, Poland, and the United States makes this case an international humanitarian concern requiring the urgent intervention of the relevant authorities and international bodies.

During the press conference, families and organizers will provide updated information on the humanitarian situation and their demands for:

Humanitarian aid and solidarity are not crimes. We call for the urgent intervention of the foreign ministries of the countries involved, the United Nations, the International Committee of the Red Cross, and all competent international organizations.
