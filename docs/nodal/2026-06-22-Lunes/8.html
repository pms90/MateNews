PONETE LA 10 | La ayuda humanitaria y la solidaridad no son delito

El Mundial 2026 se desarrolla en un contexto marcado por el genocidio contra el pueblo palestino perpetrado por Israel y por una creciente persecución de quienes impulsan acciones de solidaridad y ayuda humanitaria.

Mientras millones de personas siguen los partidos, diez integrantes del Convoy Terrestre Global Sumud Maghreb permanecen secuestrados en Libia del Este desde el 24 de mayo de 2026 por haber intentado llevar ayuda humanitaria a Gaza.

Las personas secuestradas son:

Desde hace semanas permanecen privadas arbitrariamente de su libertad, sin acceso humanitario independiente, con información limitada sobre su situación jurídica y sometidas a condiciones de aislamiento que han provocado un grave deterioro psicológico.

La solidaridad con el pueblo palestino no puede ser perseguida. Ninguna persona debe ser criminalizada por intentar llevar ayuda humanitaria.

Por eso lanzamos la campaña internacional: PONETE LA 10 | LA AYUDA HUMANITARIA NO ES DELITO

¿Por qué «Ponete la 10»?

Esta campaña busca transformar uno de los símbolos más universales del fútbol en un llamado a la solidaridad, la defensa de la vida y la dignidad humana. Mientras el mundo mira el Mundial, invitamos a no olvidar a quienes hoy siguen privados de su libertad por realizar un acto de solidaridad y al pueblo palestino que continua resistiendo.

Convocamos a medios de comunicación, periodistas, comunicadores, organizaciones sociales, sindicales, estudiantiles, políticas, culturales, deportivas, humanitarias y de derechos humanos de todo el mundo a sumarse a esta campaña internacional.

WEAR THE NUMBER 10 | Humanitarian Aid and Solidarity Are Not Crimes

The 2026 World Cup is taking place in a context marked by Israel’s genocide against the Palestinian people and by the growing persecution of those who promote acts of solidarity and humanitarian assistance.

While millions of people follow the matches, ten members of the Global Sumud Maghreb Land Convoy have remained detained in Eastern Libya since May 24, 2026, after attempting to deliver humanitarian aid to Gaza.

The detained individuals are:

For weeks, they have been arbitrarily deprived of their liberty, denied independent humanitarian access, provided with only limited information regarding their legal status, and subjected to conditions of isolation that have caused severe psychological deterioration.

Solidarity with the Palestinian people must not be persecuted. No one should be criminalized for attempting to deliver humanitarian aid.

For this reason, we are launching the international campaign:

WEAR THE NUMBER 10 | HUMANITARIAN AID AND SOLIDARITY ARE NOT CRIMES

Why “Wear the Number 10”?

Because the number 10 represents the ten detained individuals.

Because in football, wearing the number 10 means stepping up when it is needed most. It means taking responsibility, showing initiative, opening pathways, supporting the team, and rising to the occasion when the moment demands it.

And because today, in the face of the suffering of the Palestinian people and of those who tried to assist them, we cannot look the other way.

This campaign seeks to transform one of football’s most universal symbols into a call for solidarity, the defense of life, and human dignity. While the world watches the World Cup, we invite people not to forget those who remain deprived of their freedom for carrying out an act of solidarity, nor the Palestinian people who continue to resist.

We call on media outlets, journalists, communicators, social organizations, trade unions, student organizations, political movements, cultural and sports associations, humanitarian groups, and human rights organizations around the world to join this international campaign.
